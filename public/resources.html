Stress

Stress is your body's reaction to pressure or threats. In small doses it can help you perform under pressure, but chronic stress may lead to physical symptoms such as headaches, sleep problems, or irritability. To manage stress try regular exercise, time management techniques, and relaxation practices like deep breathing or progressive muscle relaxation.

Anxiety

Anxiety is a feeling of worry or fear that can be mild or overwhelming. Persistent anxiety may be associated with panic attacks, restlessness, or difficulty concentrating. Grounding techniques, limiting caffeine, and seeking support are helpful first steps. If anxiety interferes with daily life, consider talking to a professional.

Depression

Depression involves feelings of sadness, loss, or hopelessness that last for weeks or longer. It can affect your sleep, appetite, and energy levels. Reach out to loved ones, establish a routine, and engage in activities you used to enjoy. Professional help such as therapy and medication are effective treatments.

Self‑Care

Self‑care means taking actions that support your physical, emotional, and mental well‑being. Simple practices include getting enough sleep, eating well, staying active, setting boundaries, and doing things that bring you joy. Self‑care isn't selfish — it's necessary.

Coping Tips

  • Practice deep breathing or meditation daily.
  • Stay connected with friends or family.
  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule.
  • Engage in physical activity, even if it's a short walk.
  • Limit alcohol, caffeine, and social media when feeling overwhelmed.
  • Write in a journal to track feelings and triggers.

Helplines

If you are in immediate danger, call your local emergency number. Additional resources include:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (US): 988
  • Crisis Text Line (US): Text HOME to 741741
  • Samaritans (UK & Ireland): +44 (0)8457 90 90 90
  • Lifeline (Australia): 13 11 14
  • Kids Help Phone (Canada): 1-800-668-6868
